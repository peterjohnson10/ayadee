Social Entrepreneurship & Innovation

Lead Instructors: Peter Johnson & Andrew Mack

A rigorous exploration of the "Third Way," designed to help leaders deploy business logic and emerging technology toward systemic global impact.

Target Audience

Designed for young professionals and individuals transitioning from traditional business roles. This course bridges the gap between conventional ROI-driven strategy and the unique demands of social venture design.

Course Outline

Module 1 What is Social Entrepreneurship?
  • Definition and Core Pillars
  • Strategic distinctions: How it differs from typical business models
  • The Appeal: Why impact-driven models are scaling now
  • Comparative Landscape: Social Entrepreneurship vs. NGOs and CSR
  • Leveraging Technology for Social Impact
Module 2 Challenges & Strategic Traps
  • The Viability Test: Is the problem an actual business?
  • Maintaining business discipline and knowing when to pivot
  • Market Perception: Avoiding confusion in branding and presentation
Module 3 Funding & Institutional Structure
  • How to raise money for a social venture
  • Funding Ecosystems: Who funds social enterprises?
  • Working with diverse and multi-stakeholder funders
  • Structure: Balancing local roots with global scaling
Module 4 Getting Started: The 6-Month Guide

A tactical launch roadmap covering conceptualization, prototyping, and the first pilot phase.

Module 5 The Strategic Path Forward
  • Future Markets: Where impact-led business is heading
  • Analyzing Emerging Trends in the global economy
Module 6 Applied Case Studies
  • Agromovil: Tech-driven supply chain innovation
  • Microfinance: Institutional lessons and evolution
  • Global comparative analysis of successful social startups
Module 7 Self-Assessment & The Future
  • Professional Reflection: Am I a Social Entrepreneur?
  • The Macro View: Is Social Entrepreneurship the future of commerce?
