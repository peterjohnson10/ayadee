Social Entrepreneurship & Innovation

Lead Instructors: Peter Johnson & Andrew Mack

A rigorous exploration of the "Third Way," designed to help leaders deploy business logic and emerging technology toward systemic global impact.

Target Audience

Designed for young professionals and individuals transitioning from traditional business roles. This course bridges the gap between conventional ROI-driven strategy and the unique demands of social venture design.

Course Outline

Module 1 Defining the Modern Social Entrepreneur
  • Core Definitions: Moving beyond the buzzwords
  • How social ventures differ from typical business models
  • The Appeal: Why impact-driven entrepreneurship is scaling now
  • Strategic distinctions: Social Ent vs. NGOs vs. CSR
  • The Role of Technology: Scaling impact through digital leverage
Module 2 Challenges & Strategic Traps
  • The Viability Test: Is the problem actually a business?
  • Maintaining business discipline while pursuing a social mission
  • The Art of the Pivot: Adapting to market and impact realities
  • Communication Clarity: Avoiding confusion in marketing and storytelling
Module 3 Funding & Institutional Structure
  • Fundraising mechanics for the social venture
  • Investor Landscapes: Identifying the right social venture funders
  • Management: Navigating diverse and multi-stakeholder funding
  • Organizational Design: Balancing local roots with global scale
Module 4 Execution: The 6-Month Launch Guide

A tactical roadmap from conceptualization to the first pilot. Focus on lean methodology adapted for impact environments.

Module 5 Strategic Foresight: Future Markets
  • Emerging trends in global development and impact tech
  • Forecasting the next decade of "Impact Markets"
  • Assessing if Social Entrepreneurship is the optimal path for specific trends
Module 6 Applied Case Studies
  • Agromovil: Tech-driven supply chain innovation in emerging markets
  • Microfinance: Lessons from the first wave of social finance
  • Comparative analysis of high-growth social startups
Module 7 Self-Assessment & The Future

Final reflection: Evaluating your path as a social entrepreneur and analyzing if this approach represents the future of the global economy.
