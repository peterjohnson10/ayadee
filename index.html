About the Foundation

Where Foresight Meets Execution

The Ayadee Foundation operates at the intersection of strategic foresight, AI governance, digital economy strategy, and leadership development. We work with multilateral institutions, governments, universities, and private sector organizations to build the institutional capacity that responsible technology adoption requires.

Our programs draw on practitioner experience across UNDP, the European Commission, USAID, FAO, the African Development Bank, and national governments. We have delivered ecosystem assessments, policy frameworks, executive training, and governance architecture across Africa, the Middle East, Latin America, and Eastern Europe. Our research arm, Blue Shift Labs, produces applied publications on weak signal detection, scenario development, and programmable governance.

We believe the most consequential decisions of the next decade will be made by leaders who can think across systems, govern emerging technologies responsibly, and hold strategic direction under conditions of genuine uncertainty. Our work exists to develop that capacity.

Executive Education & Professional Development

Flagship Program

Strategic Foresight for the Digital Age

A comprehensive program equipping leaders with the analytical tools, scanning methodologies, and scenario frameworks required to navigate complexity and build resilient strategies. Grounded in the Three Horizons framework and the SHIFT Method, the course moves participants from theory to applied organizational capability.

Format: Live online or in-person Duration: 6 modules (adaptable: semester, 5-day intensive, or masterclass) Audience: Senior leaders, policy professionals, university faculty and students Languages: English, Spanish, French
  • Module 1: Foundations of Strategic Foresight — history, core concepts, and the Three Horizons framework for distinguishing current operations from transformative possibilities
  • Module 2: Weak Signal Detection & Horizon Scanning — systematic environmental monitoring using the STEEP-L framework across multiple time horizons, source diversification, and signal evaluation
  • Module 3: Scenario Development & Planning — from signal clustering and driving force identification to scenario construction, validation, and strategic application
  • Module 4: Digital Transformation & Emerging Technology — blockchain, AI, and platform economics as forces reshaping institutional landscapes
  • Module 5: Sustainability & Long-Term Thinking — integrating the SDGs, climate foresight, and circular economy principles into strategic planning
  • Module 6: Implementation & Organizational Change — building foresight processes, translating insights into actionable strategies, and leading through uncertainty

Institutional Capacity

AI Governance for Development Institutions

Most AI governance frameworks assume stable, high-capacity institutional environments. Development programs operate under fundamentally different conditions: complex accountability chains, inconsistent data quality, limited regulatory capacity, and high political sensitivity. This course provides the practitioner-built governance architecture that those contexts demand.

Format: Live online or in-person workshop Duration: 4 modules (2-day intensive or 4-week series) Audience: Program directors, M&E leads, donor agency staff, implementing partners Languages: English, Spanish, French
  • Module 1: Governance Architecture — the three-tier oversight model, AI use registries, use case approval processes, incident response protocols, and audit cadence design
  • Module 2: Risk Classification — a five-dimension scoring model (impact scope, decision consequence, data sensitivity, context stability, human oversight) with tiered response requirements
  • Module 3: Institutional Readiness — a six-domain diagnostic covering leadership and policy, data infrastructure, staff capacity, oversight mechanisms, partner management, and regulatory alignment
  • Module 4: Implementation & Procurement — phased roadmap from baseline assessment to scaled deployment, AI Responsible Officer role design, staffing models, and procurement standards

Leadership Development

Leadership Foundations for Public Sector & Institutional Leaders

Designed for professionals leading within universities, research environments, government agencies, and international organizations. The course establishes a shared leadership language and provides analytical tools to distinguish management from leadership, navigate adaptive challenges, and lead transformation in complex institutional settings. Currently delivered as part of the CLP Alumni Leadership & Influence Certificate at Humboldt-Universität zu Berlin.

Format: Live online (Zoom) or hybrid Duration: 3 sessions (1 hour each) + optional certification track Audience: Public sector executives, university administrators, emerging institutional leaders Languages: English, French
  • Session 1: Introduction to Leadership — leadership vs. management, self-awareness as prerequisite, the Frankl framework on purpose, and the distinction between technical and adaptive work
  • Session 2: Change Management — leading change in universities, research environments, and public institutions; frameworks for identifying resistance and planning transformation
  • Session 3: Styles of Leadership in Practice — transformational, transactional, situational, and servant leadership models; adapting approach to institutional context, culture, and team dynamics

Innovation & Creativity

Entrepreneurial Culture: Play, Creativity, Reflection & Empathy

AI tools can accelerate execution, but they cannot replace the imaginative capacity that produces original ventures, unexpected solutions, and human-centered design. This course exists because the more organizations delegate creative thinking to algorithms, the more they need to deliberately cultivate the human capabilities that algorithms cannot replicate. Participants work through structured exercises in play, creative divergence, reflective practice, and empathic inquiry, with AI tools integrated as collaborators rather than substitutes.

Format: In-person workshop or live online Duration: 4 modules (semester-length, intensive, or workshop series) Audience: Business students, startup founders, innovation teams, faculty developing entrepreneurship curricula Languages: English, Spanish, French
  • Module 1: Play — structured improvisation, rapid prototyping without judgment, and the neuroscience of creative states; using AI as a brainstorming partner while maintaining ownership of the generative process
  • Module 2: Creativity — divergent thinking techniques, design thinking sprints, and venture concept development; AI-assisted ideation with human-driven synthesis and originality testing
  • Module 3: Reflection — journaling, after-action review, and iterative self-assessment; building the metacognitive habits that prevent over-reliance on AI-generated outputs and sustain independent judgment
  • Module 4: Empathy — stakeholder immersion, user research methods, and narrative-based problem framing; understanding the human dimensions that data alone cannot capture and that AI consistently flattens
